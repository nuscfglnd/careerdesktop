Creating an
Impressive Resume

Watch and learn

It may be tempting to come up with a one size fits all resume for all your applications, but avoid this at all cost. 

Seasoned recruiters or applicant assessment software spend no more than 6 seconds scanning resumes that fit the need of the job on offer.

Be concise and selectively highlight key skills, outstanding qualities and achievements about yourself that are most relevant to the job.

01 Analyse the Job Description

Pay close attention to the job description and use competency verbs explicitly stated and inferred from the job description. Some of these competencies are commonly referred to as technical skills, while some of them are transferable skills. In addition, read between the lines and identify other skills that can be inferred.

Technical Skills

Technical skills refer to your knowledge and expertise required to perform the required
tasks or use specific tools in the role. These skills will vary across industries and functions.

Transferable Skills

Transferable skills refer to "people skills" or "self-management" skills that can be applied across careers. Your transferable skills can enhance the technical skills that you bring to the job.

While looking at the job description, highlight the key skills the employer is looking for. In addition, read between the lines and identify other skills that can be inferred.

02 List Your Experiences and Skills

Be selective and only showcase the relevant and useful ones to each specific role. Make use of verbs when listing your qualities and achievements. Include both your work experiences and Co-Curricular activities to show your interest and passion. This allows recruiters to figure out your suitability for the advertised role.

Most resumes have the following types of experiences listed. Look back at your last few years and identify experiences that fit those categories.

Work Experiences
Global and Local Internships
Overseas Exchange
Extra-Curricular Activities
Hall Involvement
Community Groups
Hobbies
Voluntary Work

In which of these experiences did you demonstrate or acquire the skills you highlighted in the job description? Those are the experiences you must prioritize in your resume.

03 Be Results-Oriented

Utilise the C.A.R. framework to structure your achievements as success stories to allow yourself to stand out from the masses. Utilising strong competencies help to improve the strength and effectiveness of your resume.

Let's review the structure behind Example 1's resume descriptor.

Effectively persuaded more than 50% of existing customers to partciipate in premium trial subscriptions

Here's how you can write your own C.A.R. statements

C

Competencies - the skill

Opt for stronger competency verbs i.e. collaborated, initiated and avoid weaker verbs i.e. support, helped

A

Actions - what you did

Explain who you have worked with and the tools that you have utilised to achieve the outcome

R

Results - what you achieved

Describe your results through quantitative i.e. $, %, time, number of attendees or qualitative outcomes.

This is how you can demonstrate your accomplishments in a clear and impactful manner.

Before After
Update social media account Designed and introduced marketing collaterals such as customer interviews and product infographics on our social media account thereby increasing web traffic by 10%.
Promote electronic goods Exceeded monthly sales target by 15% and was awarded the Best Sales Person of the Month.
Support the marketing team Grew the customer base by implementing innovative public relations campaign with the marketing team to promote the latest digital products.

04 Applying Finishing Touches

How you present your resume is as important as what is written on it. A well-formatted resume is easy on the eyes and allows the recruiter to quickly spot the competencies you have and the results you have achieved. Recruiters are quickly turned off by poorly formatted resumes and typos.

Sections within a Resume

  1. Name and Contact Details
  2. Education
  3. Work Experience
  4. CCA/External Projects
  5. Additional Skills/Interests

Formatting Tips

  • One Page
  • All Dates in MMM YY format
  • All bullets to be properly aligned across the entire document
  • Black font with white background, balance of text and space
  • Simple, formal and readable font (Arial, Times New Roman, Verdana - Font Size: 11/12)
