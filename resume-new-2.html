It may be tempting to come up with a one size fits all resume for all your applications, but avoid this at all cost. 

Crafting a tailored resume that lists your outstanding qualities and achievements will help you to differentiate yourself from the competition. By highlighting key skills that are most relevant to the job, you will stand a higher chance of being selected.

01 Analyse the Job Description

Pay close attention to the job description and use strong action verbs to highlight the competency that is explicitly stated and inferred from the job description. Some of these competencies are commonly referred to as technical skills, while some of them are transferable skills. In addition, read between the lines and identify other skills that can be inferred.

Technical Skills

Technical skills refer to your knowledge and expertise required to perform the required tasks or use specific tools in the role. These skills will vary across industries and functions.

Transferable Skills

Transferable skills refer to "people skills" or "self-management" skills that can be applied across careers. Your transferable skills can enhance the technical skills that you bring to the job.

02 List Your Experiences and Skills

Be selective and only showcase the relevant and useful ones to each specific role. Make use of verbs when listing your qualities and achievements. Include both your work experiences and Co-Curricular activities to show your interest and passion. This allows recruiters to figure out your suitability for the advertised role.

Most resumes have the following types of experiences listed. Look back at your last few years and identify experiences that fit those categories.

Work Experiences
Global and Local Internships
Overseas Exchange
Extra-Curricular Activities
Hall Involvement
Community Groups
Hobbies
Voluntary Work

In which of these experiences did you demonstrate or acquire the skills you highlighted in the job description? Those are the experiences you must prioritize in your resume. 

03 Be Results-Oriented

Utilise the C.A.R. framework to structure your achievements as success stories to allow yourself to stand out from the masses. Using strong competency statements help to improve the strength and effectiveness of your resume.

Writing C.A.R. Statements

C

Competency

Competencies can be grouped into various categories such as Communication skills, Creative skills, Leadership skills etc.

 Opt for strong action verbs such as collaborated and initiated to highlight the competency at the start of the sentence.

 Avoid weak verbs such as assisted and supported.

The following examples of strong action verbs allow you to highlight your competencies.

  • Communication skills - negotiated, collaborated
  • Creative skills - conceptualized, designed
  • Leadership skills - spearheaded, led

A

Action

Explain who you have worked with and the tools that you have utilised to achieve the outcome or desired goal.

R

Results

Express the significant quantitative or qualitative outcome of your action.

Quantify the results wherever possible by using % increase in sales, the number of customers, grades achieved).

For qualitative results, you can state the purpose of your action. For example, "to increase sales in the next quarter" or "to investigate the role of market supply in consumer behaviour".

This is how you can demonstrate your accomplishments in a clear and impactful manner.

Before

Update social media account

Promote electronic goods

Support the marketing team

After

Designed and introduced marketing collaterals such as customer interviews and product infographics on our social media account thereby increasing web traffic by 10%

Exceeded monthly sales target by 15% and was awarded the Best Sales Person of the Month

Grew the customer base by implementing innovative public relations campaign with the marketing team to promote the latest digital products

04 Apply Finishing Touches

How you present your resume is as important as what is written on it. A well-formatted resume is easy on the eyes and allows the recruiter to quickly spot the competencies you have and the results you have achieved. Recruiters are quickly turned off by poorly formatted resumes and typos.

Presenting Your Resume

Resume Format

A reverse chronological order resume is recommended for fresh graduates to:

  • tailor different qualification and skill sets to each specific role, and
  • highlight past work experiences and achievements, starting with the most recent

Sections

  1. Name and Contact Details
  2. Education
  3. Work Experience
  4. CCA/External Projects
  5. Additional Skills/Interests

Presentation Tips

  • One Page
  • All Dates in MMM YY format
  • All bullets to be properly aligned across the entire document
  • Black font with white background, balance of text and space
  • Simple, formal and readable font (Arial, Times New Roman, Verdana - Font Size: 11/12)
